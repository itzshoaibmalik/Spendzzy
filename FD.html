Fixed Deposit

1.   What is FD: FD is a type of investment where you deposit a lump sum amount in a bank for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate.

2.   Tenure: You choose the tenure (e.g., 1 year, 5 years) during which your money remains locked.

3.   Interest: Banks pay you interest on your deposit. The rate is fixed when you open the FD.

4.   Safety: FDs are considered safe because they are backed by the bank, often insured, and not subject to market fluctuations.

5.   Liquidity: While your money is locked for the chosen tenure, you can opt for a premature withdrawal with a penalty.

6.   Interest Payout: You can choose to receive interest periodically (monthly, quarterly) or at maturity.

7.   Taxation: Interest earned on FDs is taxable as per your income tax slab.

8.   Reinvestment: You can reinvest the maturity amount in another FD or withdraw it.

9.   Goal-Oriented: FDs are ideal for short to medium-term goals and for preserving capital.

10.   Diversify: Consider diversifying your investments for better returns and liquidity.
