Budgeting in finance is a critical tool for managing your money wisely. Here's a concise and precise guide for beginners:

1.   Track Your Income: Know how much moneyyou earn monthly or annually after taxes.

2.   List Your Expenses: Record all your regular expenses, including housing, groceries, transportation, utilities, and entertainment and Categorize them.

3.   Create a Budget: Allocate a specific amount of your income to each expense category. Ensure your total expenses are less than your income.

4.   Emergency Fund: Set aside a portion of your income for unexpected expenses in an emergency fund.

5.   Save and Invest: Allocate a portion of your budget for savings and investments to build wealth over time. Track your spendings and keep a check if there is any need to adjust or not.

6.   Pay Down Debt: Prioritize paying off high-interest debts to reduce financial stress.

7.   Automate Savings: Set up automatic transfers to your savings or investment accounts to ensure consistency.
